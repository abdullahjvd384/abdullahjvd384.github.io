Types of Car Insurance Coverage: Full vs Liability-Only

Liability Coverage (Bodily Injury and Property Damage Insurance)

Liability covers damages you cause to others. Most states require minimum liability limits; however, these minimums are often too low to fully protect you after a serious accident. Cheap car insurance plans often only include state minimums, but upgrading to higher liability limits (100/300/100) provides better protection for just $10-20 more per month.

Collision and Comprehensive Coverage (Full Coverage Auto Insurance)

Collision covers damage to your vehicle in a crash; comprehensive covers non-collision losses like theft, vandalism, or weather-related damage. Together, they form full coverage car insurance, which is required if you have an auto loan or lease.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage

Protects you if the at-fault driver lacks sufficient insurance. This is critical coverage since 13% of US drivers are uninsured, and it's often available for just $5-10/month extra on your auto insurance quote.

How Car Insurance Rates Are Calculated (And How to Lower Them)

Insurance companies use a combination of factors: driving history, age, vehicle type, credit-based insurance score (where permitted), zip code, annual mileage, and coverage selections.

12 Proven Ways to Get Cheap Car Insurance and Save Money

  1. Compare quotes annually from at least three carriers.
  2. Bundle auto and home insurance for multi-policy discounts.
  3. Increase deductibles where affordable.
  4. Maintain a clean driving record; take defensive driving courses for discounts.
  5. Use telematics or usage-based insurance programs if you drive under 10k miles/year.
  6. Review your coverage limits and drop unnecessary add-ons.
  7. Improve vehicle security (alarm, VIN etching) to lower theft surcharges.
  8. Park in a garage when possible.
  9. Check for employer, alumni, or association discounts.
  10. Pay annually instead of monthly to avoid service fees.
  11. Shop for insurance after major life events (marriage, relocation).
  12. Consider insuring multiple vehicles with the same carrier for fleet discounts.

Frequently asked questions

1. What level of liability coverage should I choose?

Choose limits above state minimums. Common recommended minimums are 100/300/100 (in many states) but consult your agent for personal risk.

2. Does my credit score affect my insurance rate?

In most states, insurers may use credit-based insurance scores; a better score often yields lower premiums. Some states (e.g., California, Massachusetts) restrict this practice.

3. How do I file a discount for safety equipment?

Contact your insurer with proof of equipment or installation receipts. Many carriers have online portals for discounts.
